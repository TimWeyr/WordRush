SPAWN DISTRIBUTION EXAMPLES
===========================

Visual representation of how objects are distributed across the screen.
Screen width: 0.0 (left edge) to 1.0 (right edge)
Safe zone: 0.15 to 0.85 (to avoid edge spawning)

-------------------------
Screen: |------------[●]------------|
        0.0          0.5          1.0
        
Position: 0.5 (center)
Spread: 0.05 (small jitter)

TEXT WIDTH IMPACT
=================

Short Words (e.g., "I", "a", "PC")
-----------------------------------
Minimum distance: 0.08
Can be closer together
Example: [I]--[a]--[PC]


Normal Words (e.g., "mouse", "keyboard")
----------------------------------------
Minimum distance: 0.12
Standard spacing
Example: [mouse]----[keyboard]


Long Words (e.g., "Geschäftsführung", "administration")
-------------------------------------------------------
Minimum distance: 0.18
Extra spacing to prevent overlap
Example: [Geschäftsführung]--------[administration]

REAL-WORLD EXAMPLE: Business English
=====================================

Item: Business Communication Level 1
Base: "email"

Corrects (3):
  "message"     → 0.38, spread 0.06
  "letter"      → 0.50, spread 0.05
  "memo"        → 0.62, spread 0.07

Distractors (4):
  "Brief"       → 0.33, spread 0.08
  "Nachricht"   → 0.45, spread 0.07
  "Post"        → 0.57, spread 0.08
  "Mitteilung"  → 0.70, spread 0.09

Visual:
Screen: |--[Brief][message]--[Nachricht][letter][memo][Post]---[Mitteilung]--|
        0.15      0.33  0.38    0.45     0.50  0.57 0.62  0.70        0.85

Result: Natural distribution, no linear pattern, good spacing

EDGE CASES
==========

All Short Words:
Screen: |-----[I][a][PC][OS][UI][UX]-----|
        (tighter spacing, still distributed)

All Long Words:
Screen: |-[administration]----[communication]----[presentation]-|
        (wider spacing, fewer fit on screen)

Mixed Lengths:
Screen: |--[I]--[communication]--[PC]--[presentation]--[a]--|
        (adaptive spacing based on neighbors)

GAMEPLAY IMPACT
===============

Small Group (3-5 objects):
  Player stays near center
  Easy to collect all
  Low difficulty

Medium Group (6-10 objects):
  Player moves left-right
  Moderate challenge
  Some prioritization needed

Large Group (>10 objects):
  Full screen coverage
  High difficulty
  Strategic collection required


NOTES
=====

- All positions are normalized (0.0 to 1.0)
- Actual pixel positions calculated at runtime based on screen width
- Spread adds randomness within range (prevents exact positioning)
- Clusters prevent linear "row" appearance
- Algorithm runs in editor, not during gameplay (no performance impact)
- Positions saved to database, used by ShooterEngine at spawn time
